Interwoven Feedback

×

This webpage prototype represents an abstract 2.5D diorama inspired by Wassily Kandisky’s abstract art studies. I chose this art style, and Kandisky’s works in particular, because I believe they are a meaningful source of inspiration and starting point to create a simple yet effective interactive environment. There are plenty of affordances in these art references, to create abstract compositions that users can interact with to receive creative and effective feedback. When interacting with each shape, users will receive audiovisual feedback appropriate to the size, position and nature of that element (each visual element on the page plays an appropriate and specific audio track when users hover or click on that element.). Amongst the many shapes shown in the original painting, I only chose those that could actually provide feedback on mouse hover or on click. In particular, I chose circles, triangles and squares that could rotate, roll, translate, bounce and produce sounds as well. I then mixed these elements together to create an aesthetically cohesive layout that reflects the chosen art style, so users can better navigate and enjoy the creative composition. I chose sounds that are copyright free and have also been edited in Reaper Daw, to make them sound realistic and diegetic, in relation to position, size and material of the visual object producing that sound. In order to fake how spatial audio sounds like, and to deliver a better user experience and feedback, I added some post-production sound effects like reverb, panning, gain and volume. This mimics the spatiality of sound in a real 3D environment, while rendering on the web an audio format that most users can have access to and easily load on their browsers. Interactive audio assets of the page include: • Earcons and hard effects that mimic real materials, like glass and plastic, movement sounds (like rolling and bouncing). I believe that, compared to the previous one, this audiovisual prototype better reflects the chosen pair. Audio and visuals interweave together in a playful way to convey audiovisual feedback. Compared to my first prototype, this one contains audiovisual interactions that work better together creatively and convey meaningful feedback.
